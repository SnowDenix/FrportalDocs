## Purpose

This policy defines the rules for collecting, processing, storing, and protecting customer personal data at Aydınlı Grup franchise stores (U.S. Polo Assn., Pierre Cardin, Cacharel) within the framework of GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) and applicable local data protection laws.

### Policy Objectives

- Ensure full GDPR compliance
- Manage customer data securely
- Standardize data processing procedures
- Protect and respect customer rights
- Minimize data breach risks

## Definitions

### GDPR Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Personal Data** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person |
| **Special Category Data** | Race, health, biometric data, and other sensitive data |
| **Data Controller** | Entity determining the purposes and means of data processing (Aydınlı Grup) |
| **Data Processor** | Entity processing data on behalf of the controller |
| **Explicit Consent** | Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of agreement |
| **Privacy Notice** | Information explaining to customers how their data will be processed |
| **Erasure** | Deletion, destruction, or anonymization of personal data |
| **Data Breach** | Unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure of personal data |

### Consent Types

| Consent Type | Description | Legal Basis |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Data Processing Consent** | Approval for processing personal data | GDPR Art. 6 |
| **Marketing Consent** | SMS/Email marketing permission | GDPR Art. 7 |
| **Profiling Consent** | Customer behavior analysis | GDPR Art. 22 |
| **Third Party Sharing Consent** | Sharing data with partners | GDPR Art. 6 |

## Data Collection Principles

### Data That May Be Collected

| Data | Purpose | Requirement |
|------|---------|-------------|
| First Name, Last Name | Customer identification | Required |
| Mobile Phone | Communication, verification | Required |
| Email | Communication, digital services | Recommended |
| Date of Birth | Birthday campaigns | Optional |
| Gender | Personalized service | Optional |
| Address | Delivery services | Optional |

### Data That Must NOT Be Collected

The following data **must never be collected**:

| Data Type | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Health information | Illness, medication use, etc. |
| Religious belief | Religion, denomination |
| Political opinion | Party membership, etc. |
| Race/Ethnicity | Ethnic origin |
| Biometric data | Fingerprint, facial recognition |
| Criminal conviction | Criminal record |
| Sexual life | Sexual preference |

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Asking for, recording, or processing this data is prohibited and subject to severe penalties.

### Data Collection Rules

1. **Collect only necessary data:** Don't collect data beyond purpose
2. **Provide information:** Explain why data is collected
3. **Obtain consent:** Document explicit consent
4. **Verify:** Confirm accuracy of entered data
5. **Keep secure:** Protect data from unauthorized access

## Consent Management

### Privacy Notice Presentation

**When:** At every new customer registration

**How:**
1. Have customer read from digital screen
2. Or provide printed text
3. Confirm customer has read/understood

**Privacy Notice Content:**
- Identity of the data controller
- Purposes of data processing
- Recipients of data
- Data collection method and legal basis
- Customer rights under GDPR

## Customer Rights

### Rights Under GDPR

| Right | Description | Timeframe |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Right to be Informed** | Learn whether data is being processed | 30 days |
| **Right of Access** | Request access to processed data | 30 days |
| **Right to Rectification** | Request correction of incomplete/incorrect data | 30 days |
| **Right to Erasure** | Request deletion of data (right to be forgotten) | 30 days |
| **Right to Data Portability** | Receive data in machine-readable format | 30 days |
| **Right to Object** | Object to automated decision results | 30 days |
| **Right to Compensation** | Claim compensation if harmed | Legal process |

## Process Steps

### 1. Data Collection at New Customer Registration

**Step 1:** Present privacy notice to customer

**Step 2:** Confirm text has been read/understood

**Step 3:** Obtain data processing consent (required)

**Step 4:** Ask about marketing communication consents (optional)

**Step 5:** Record consents in CRM

**Step 6:** If physical form, have signed and archive

**Step 7:** Inform the customer

> ✅ **Correct:** "Dear customer, we need your consent to process your information to serve you better. This data will be protected under GDPR and used only to provide service. You can also opt-in to receive campaign updates."

> ❌ **Wrong:** "Sign here" (without explanation)

---

### 2. Customer Information Update

**Step 1:** Verify customer identity

**Step 2:** Obtain information to be updated

**Step 3:** Record change in CRM

**Step 4:** Give customer update confirmation

---

### 3. Erasure/Right to be Forgotten Request

**Step 1:** Receive written application from customer

**Step 2:** Open ticket (Category: GDPR Request)

**Step 3:** Verify customer identity

**Step 4:** Forward to CRM Headquarters

**Step 5:** Check legal retention obligations

**Step 6:** Delete data that can be deleted

**Step 7:** Provide written response within 30 days

> ⚠️ **Note:** Some data cannot be deleted due to legal obligations (e.g., invoice data must be kept for 10 years).

---

### 4. In Case of Data Breach

**Step 1:** Detect and stop the breach

**Step 2:** Inform the store manager

**Step 3:** Make urgent notification to CRM Headquarters

**Step 4:** Document breach details:
- When?
- Which data?
- How many customers affected?
- How did it happen?

**Step 5:** CRM HQ notifies supervisory authority (within 72 hours)

**Step 6:** Affected customers are notified

## Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: Customer Wants to Withdraw Consent

**Solution:**
1. Receive request in writing
2. If data processing consent withdrawn, record is deactivated not deleted
3. Marketing consent cancelled immediately
4. Provide written response within 30 days

---

### Issue 2: Customer Wants to Query Their Data

**Solution:**
1. Verify customer identity
2. Extract customer data from CRM
3. Deliver to customer in writing
4. Get delivery acknowledgment

---

### Issue 3: Customer Complains About Incorrect Data

**Solution:**
1. Verify the error
2. Get correct information from customer
3. Correct in CRM
4. Give customer correction confirmation

---

### Issue 4: Suspected Data Breach

**Solution:**
1. Don't panic but act quickly
2. Block unauthorized access
3. Inform store manager
4. Call CRM Headquarters
5. Don't delete/change anything (evidence)

## Store Tips

### Correct Practices

| Do | Description |
|----|-------------|
| ✅ Always provide information | Don't process data without consent |
| ✅ Document consents | Keep digital or physical records |
| ✅ Lock your screen | When leaving your desk |
| ✅ Protect your password | Don't share with anyone |
| ✅ Report suspicious situations | Immediately inform manager |

### Incorrect Practices

| Don't | Risk |
|-------|------|
| ❌ Process data without consent | GDPR penalty |
| ❌ Ask for sensitive data | Legal sanction |
| ❌ Share data with others | Data breach |
| ❌ Leave customer screen open | Unauthorized access |
| ❌ Write customer info on paper | Data security risk |

### Customer Explanation Examples

**When asking for consent:**
> "Dear customer, we need to record some of your information to serve you better. This information will be protected under GDPR and used only to provide service to you. You can also opt-in to be informed about our campaigns."

**If they don't want to give consent:**
> "Of course, this is entirely your choice. In this case, we can only make a receipt-only sale, and you won't be able to benefit from loyalty card advantages."

**If they want to query their information:**
> "Certainly, you have this right under GDPR. After we receive your written application, we will provide your information within 30 days."
