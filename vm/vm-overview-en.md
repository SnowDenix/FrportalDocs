## Purpose

This document provides a comprehensive overview of Visual Merchandising (VM) principles, standards, and execution guidelines for Aydınlı Group franchise partners operating U.S. Polo Assn., Pierre Cardin, and Cacharel brand stores globally.

### VM Mission Statement

Visual Merchandising transforms retail spaces into immersive brand experiences that:
- **Inspire** customers through compelling product presentations
- **Guide** shopping journeys with strategic product placement
- **Convert** visitors into buyers through visual storytelling
- **Build** brand loyalty through consistent, premium experiences

## Definitions

| Term (EN) | Term (TR) | Definition |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Visual Merchandising (VM) | Görsel Mağazacılık | The practice of developing floor plans and displays to maximize sales |
| Planogram | Planogram | A diagram showing how and where retail products should be placed |
| Focal Point | Odak Noktası | The main point of visual interest that draws customer attention |
| Hero Product | Kahraman Ürün | Featured product that drives traffic and represents the season |
| Cross-Merchandising | Çapraz Sergileme | Displaying complementary products together |
| Color Story | Renk Hikayesi | Coordinated color palette used across displays |
| Capacity | Kapasite | Maximum number of products on a fixture |
| Facing | Cephe | Number of identical products visible from the front |
| Adjacency | Komşuluk | Strategic placement of related product categories |
| Sight Line | Görüş Hattı | Customer's natural viewing path through the store |
| Decompression Zone | Dekompresyon Alanı | Entry area where customers adjust to the store environment |
| A/B/C Zones | A/B/C Bölgeleri | Store areas ranked by customer traffic and sales potential |
| POP (Point of Purchase) | Satın Alma Noktası | Marketing materials placed near merchandise |
| POSM | Satış Noktası Materyali | Point of Sale Materials for promotional purposes |
| Gondola | Gondol | Free-standing double-sided fixture |
| Nesting Table | İç İçe Masa | Stackable display tables of varying heights |

## Standards & Rules

### Core VM Principles

1. **Golden Triangle Rule**
   - Primary focal point visible from entrance
   - Hero products in premium sight lines
   - Maximum 3 focal points per view

2. **Eye Level Priority**
   - Premium zone: 145-170 cm (adult eye level)
   - Touch zone: 90-145 cm (easy reach)
   - Lower zone: 0-90 cm (bulk items)
   - Upper zone: 170+ cm (stock/decor)

3. **Right-Turn Principle**
   - 90% of customers naturally turn right
   - Position new season/hero products on right
   - Create clockwise traffic flow

4. **Decompression Zone**
   - First 1-2 meters from entrance
   - No critical products in this area
   - Use for brand ambiance

### Mandatory Standards

| Standard | Requirement | Tolerance |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Window change frequency | Per calendar | ±2 days |
| Mannequin condition | No damage/marks | Zero tolerance |
| Lighting functionality | 100% operational | 24-hour fix |
| Price tag visibility | On all products | Zero tolerance |
| Size organization | XS→XXL left to right | Zero tolerance |
| Fixture capacity | Per product type | ±10% |
| Color blocking | Gradient arrangement | As per guide |
| Signage accuracy | Current campaign only | Zero tolerance |

### Prohibited Practices

- ❌ Overcrowded fixtures exceeding capacity
- ❌ Mixed brand displays (unless approved)
- ❌ Handwritten signage
- ❌ Damaged/dirty fixtures or props
- ❌ Outdated campaign materials
- ❌ Empty mannequins or fixtures
- ❌ Inconsistent hanger types
- ❌ Products on floor
- ❌ Blocked sight lines
- ❌ Non-approved fixtures

## Examples / Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Season Launch

**Situation:** Spring/Summer collection launch day

**VM Execution Plan:**
```
Timeline: T-7 to T+1

T-7: Receive VM kit, verify inventory
T-5: Team briefing, assign roles
T-3: Back-of-house prep, steam products
T-1: Evening: Begin window/store transformation
T-0: 06:00-09:00 Complete execution
     09:30 Grand reveal
T+1: Photo documentation, issue resolution
```

**Success Metrics:**
- Window change completed by store opening
- All hero products in A-zone
- Photo documentation uploaded within 24 hours
- Zero VM compliance issues

### Scenario 2: Campaign Period VM Adaptation

**Situation:** Black Friday promotion starting

**Required Changes:**
1. Window: Add promotional signage overlay
2. Entrance: Install campaign floor graphics
3. In-store: Position promotional signage at key points
4. Cash wrap: Add impulse buy displays
5. Fitting room: Add upsell signage

**Execution Timeline:** 24 hours before campaign start

### Scenario 3: Low Stock Situation

**Situation:** Key products running low before restock

**VM Solution:**
```
Option A: Consolidation
- Combine colors on fewer fixtures
- Maintain visual density
- Create "curated selection" story

Option B: Substitution
- Replace with similar style
- Maintain color story
- Update mannequin accordingly

Option C: Re-merchandising
- Expand adjacent category
- Create new cross-merchandising opportunity
- Highlight available alternatives
```

## Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: Overcrowded Fixtures

**Problem:** Products packed too tightly, reducing visual appeal

**Solution:**
- Remove excess stock to back room
- Follow capacity guidelines strictly
- Create "breathing space" between products
- Use negative space intentionally

**Prevention:** Daily capacity checks, proper stock rotation

---

### Issue 2: Inconsistent Window Execution

**Problem:** Window doesn't match HQ guidelines

**Solution:**
- Compare with reference images step-by-step
- Request video tutorial from HQ
- Schedule VM team visit for training
- Document correct execution for future

**Prevention:** Pre-change briefing, reference photo board

---

### Issue 3: Mannequin Styling Errors

**Problem:** Incorrect outfit combinations or poor fit

**Solution:**
- Review look book styling guide
- Use approved outfit combinations only
- Ensure proper sizing for mannequin type
- Pin/tape for perfect fit
- Replace damaged mannequin pieces

**Prevention:** Weekly mannequin styling review

---

### Issue 4: Color Story Breakdown

**Problem:** Color blocks become mixed during selling

**Solution:**
- Implement hourly "reset walks"
- Assign specific zones to team members
- Use zone photos as reference
- Customer-proof with extra facing

**Prevention:** Visual zone maps at back of house

---

### Issue 5: Signage Errors

**Problem:** Wrong prices, outdated campaigns, or missing tags

**Solution:**
- Immediate price audit
- Remove all outdated materials
- Check against current campaign brief
- Report systematic issues to HQ

**Prevention:** Pre-campaign signage checklist, date-stamp all materials

---

### Issue 6: Lighting Problems

**Problem:** Burnt bulbs, poor focus, wrong color temperature

**Solution:**
- Replace bulbs within 24 hours
- Adjust spotlight angles during off-peak
- Request maintenance for complex issues
- Document recurring problems

**Prevention:** Weekly lighting walk, spare bulb inventory
