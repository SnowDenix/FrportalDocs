## Purpose

This document establishes comprehensive customer service standards for all Aydınlı Group franchise stores. Exceptional customer service differentiates our brands, builds loyalty, and drives sustainable business growth.

### Service Vision

"Every customer leaves feeling valued, inspired, and eager to return."

### Service Pillars

| Pillar | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Welcome** | Every customer acknowledged within 30 seconds |
| **Engage** | Genuine conversation, understanding needs |
| **Inspire** | Style advice, complete outfit solutions |
| **Serve** | Efficient, accurate, thoughtful service |
| **Thank** | Sincere appreciation and invitation to return |

---

## Scope

These standards apply to:
- All customer interactions (in-store, phone, digital)
- All staff levels (sales associates to managers)
- All store formats and locations
- All brands (U.S. Polo Assn., Pierre Cardin, Cacharel)

### Service Interaction Framework

```
ASK - LISTEN - RECOMMEND - CONFIRM

1. ASK
   └── Open-ended questions to understand needs
       • "What occasion are you shopping for?"
       • "What style do you usually prefer?"
       • "Is this for yourself or a gift?"

2. LISTEN
   └── Active listening with genuine interest
       • Maintain eye contact
       • Nod and acknowledge
       • Don't interrupt
       • Note preferences and constraints

3. RECOMMEND
   └── Tailored suggestions based on needs
       • Match products to stated needs
       • Explain why recommendation fits
       • Offer alternatives
       • Be honest about fit/style

4. CONFIRM
   └── Validate the choice
       • Summarize what customer wants
       • Confirm sizes needed
       • Check if anything else needed
       • Reinforce good choice
```

## Standards & Rules

### Response Time Standards

| Interaction | Maximum Time | Target |
|-------------|--------------|--------|
| Initial greeting | 30 seconds | 15 seconds |
| Assistance request | 60 seconds | 30 seconds |
| Fitting room check | 5 minutes | 3 minutes |
| Checkout wait | 3 minutes | Under 2 minutes |
| Phone answer | 3 rings | 2 rings |
| Customer complaint | Immediate | Immediate |

### Service Behaviors

#### Required Behaviors ✓

- ✓ Smile and make eye contact
- ✓ Use customer's name when known
- ✓ Thank customer at multiple touchpoints
- ✓ Offer to help carry items
- ✓ Walk customer to product, don't point
- ✓ Escort to fitting room
- ✓ Offer alternative sizes proactively
- ✓ Follow up at fitting room
- ✓ Suggest complete outfits
- ✓ Offer bag at checkout

#### Prohibited Behaviors ✗

- ✗ Ignoring customers
- ✗ Chatting with colleagues when customers present
- ✗ Using mobile phones on floor
- ✗ Eating or drinking on sales floor
- ✗ Negative body language (crossed arms, leaning)
- ✗ Arguing with customers
- ✗ Making assumptions about customers
- ✗ Pressuring or following customers
- ✗ Discussing personal matters
- ✗ Criticizing competitors

### Communication Standards

| Aspect | Standard |
|--------|----------|
| **Tone** | Warm, professional, genuine |
| **Language** | Clear, jargon-free, respectful |
| **Volume** | Appropriate to environment |
| **Pace** | Moderate, allowing processing |
| **Body Language** | Open, engaged, attentive |

## Examples / Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Busy Store, Single Customer Waiting

**Situation:** Store is busy, customer waiting for assistance

**Response:**
```
1. Acknowledge immediately (eye contact + nod)
2. Say: "I'll be with you in just a moment!"
3. Finish current interaction efficiently
4. Approach waiting customer promptly
5. Apologize for wait
6. Give full attention
```

### Scenario 2: Indecisive Customer

**Situation:** Customer can't decide between two items

**Response:**
```
1. Understand the hesitation
   "What's making the decision difficult?"
   
2. Highlight differences
   "This one is more versatile for different occasions,
    while this is perfect for [specific use]."
    
3. Ask about use case
   "Where do you see yourself wearing this most?"
   
4. Offer reassurance
   "Both are great choices. Our return policy gives you
    time to decide at home if needed."
    
5. If still undecided
   "Would you like to take both and return the one
    that doesn't work?"
```

### Scenario 3: Customer Complaint

**Situation:** Customer unhappy with previous purchase

**Response:**
```
1. LISTEN
   - Let customer explain fully
   - Don't interrupt
   - Show empathy
   
2. ACKNOWLEDGE
   "I completely understand your frustration.
    That's not the experience we want you to have."
    
3. APOLOGIZE
   "I'm sorry this happened. Let me make it right."
   
4. SOLVE
   - Offer solution within authority
   - If beyond authority, involve manager
   - Act quickly
   
5. THANK
   "Thank you for giving us the chance to fix this.
    We really value your feedback."
```

## Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: Customers Leaving Without Assistance

**Problem:** Customers leave without staff engagement

**Solutions:**
- Improve floor coverage
- Position staff at key zones
- Train on reading buying signals
- Implement greeting accountability
- Use door chime/counter awareness

---

### Issue 2: Low Conversion Despite Traffic

**Problem:** Many visitors but few purchases

**Solutions:**
- Better needs discovery
- More fitting room conversion focus
- Improve product knowledge
- Train on overcoming objections
- Enhance outfit suggestion skills

---

### Issue 3: Customer Complaints About Wait Times

**Problem:** Customers frustrated by waiting

**Solutions:**
- Acknowledge waiting customers immediately
- Implement queue management
- Cross-train for checkout backup
- Schedule peak hours appropriately
- Communicate expected wait time
