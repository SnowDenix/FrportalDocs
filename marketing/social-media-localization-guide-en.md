## Purpose

This document provides comprehensive guidelines for localizing social media content across different markets while maintaining brand consistency. It ensures effective communication with local audiences while respecting cultural nuances and regional preferences.

### Localization Objectives

| Objective | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Relevance** | Create content that resonates with local audiences |
| **Consistency** | Maintain brand identity across all markets |
| **Engagement** | Maximize interaction with localized content |
| **Compliance** | Adhere to local regulations and cultural norms |
| **Efficiency** | Streamline localization processes |

## Language Guidelines

### Translation vs. Transcreation

| Approach | When to Use | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct Translation** | Factual information, prices | "50% OFF" → "خصم 50%" |
| **Transcreation** | Slogans, emotional content | "Live Your Style" → locally resonant phrase |
| **Keep English** | Brand names, hashtags, tech terms | "U.S. Polo Assn." stays same |

### Arabic Content Guidelines

**General Rules:**
- Right-to-left text alignment
- Use Modern Standard Arabic or local dialect as appropriate
- Formal tone for brand communications
- Consider text expansion (Arabic often longer)

**Typography:**
- Use approved Arabic fonts
- Maintain readability at small sizes
- Test on multiple devices

### French Content Guidelines (Morocco)

**General Rules:**
- Formal "vous" for brand communications
- Fashion terminology in French acceptable
- Balance with Arabic for broader reach

### Multi-Language Posts

**Strategy:**
- Lead with local language
- English as secondary (optional)
- Avoid mixing languages mid-sentence
- Use line breaks between languages

## Community Management

### Response Time Standards

| Message Type | Target Response Time |
|--------------|---------------------|
| **Complaints** | Within 2 hours |
| **Questions** | Within 4 hours |
| **Comments (positive)** | Within 24 hours |
| **Comments (neutral)** | Within 24 hours |
| **DMs** | Within 4 hours |

### Response Language Guidelines

**General Principles:**
- Respond in the language used by the customer
- Maintain brand tone while being personable
- Never argue publicly
- Move sensitive issues to DM

**Response Templates (Adapt for language):**

| Situation | Response Template |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Thank You** | "Thank you for your kind words! We're so happy you love [product]. 💙" |
| **Question** | "Great question! [Answer]. Feel free to DM us if you need more info!" |
| **Complaint** | "We're sorry to hear this. Please DM us your details so we can help resolve this." |
| **Store Inquiry** | "You can find us at [location]. Visit us today! [Store hours]" |

### Escalation Protocol

```
Level 1: Standard Response (Store/Regional Team)
├── Product questions
├── Store information
├── General compliments
└── Simple complaints

Level 2: Regional Manager Review
├── Persistent complaints
├── Negative reviews
├── Influencer interactions
└── Sensitive topics

Level 3: HQ Escalation
├── Brand reputation issues
├── Legal concerns
├── PR crises
└── Influencer partnerships
```

## Process Steps

### Content Localization Process

```
Step 1: Receive Global Content
        ↓
Step 2: Review for Local Relevance
        ↓
Step 3: Identify Adaptation Needs
        └── Language, visuals, timing, offers
        ↓
Step 4: Perform Translation/Transcreation
        ↓
Step 5: Cultural Review
        ↓
Step 6: Visual Adaptation (if needed)
        ↓
Step 7: Regional Manager Approval
        ↓
Step 8: Schedule and Publish
        ↓
Step 9: Monitor and Engage
        ↓
Step 10: Report Performance
```

### Local Content Creation Process

```
1. Identify Local Opportunity
   └── Event, trend, or store activity
       ↓
2. Create Content Brief
   └── Objective, format, timing
       ↓
3. Submit for Regional Approval
   └── Brand compliance check
       ↓
4. Create Content
   └── Using approved templates/assets
       ↓
5. Final Review
   └── Language, visuals, compliance
       ↓
6. Publish
       ↓
7. Monitor and Engage
```

## Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: Low Engagement on Localized Content

**Symptom:** Localized posts performing below average.

**Causes:**
- Translation not resonating
- Cultural mismatch
- Poor timing
- Irrelevant content

**Solutions:**
1. Review transcreation quality
2. Conduct local audience research
3. Test different posting times
4. Analyze top-performing local content
5. Incorporate more UGC

---

### Issue 2: Negative Comments in Local Language

**Symptom:** Complaints or criticism in local language.

**Solutions:**
1. Translate and understand the issue
2. Respond promptly in same language
3. Move to DM for resolution
4. Escalate if serious
5. Document for pattern analysis

---

### Issue 3: Cultural Misstep

**Symptom:** Content receiving negative feedback for cultural reasons.

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Remove content immediately
2. Assess severity and reach
3. Prepare appropriate response
4. Escalate to HQ if significant
5. Document learnings

**Prevention:**
- Enhanced cultural review process
- Local team consultation
- Cultural calendar awareness
- Regular team training
